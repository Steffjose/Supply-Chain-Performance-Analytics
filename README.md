# Supply-Chain-Performance-Analytics
End-to-End Supply Chain Analytics Platform for Cost Optimization, Demand Forecasting, and Supplier Risk Management.

Project at a Glance
This project solves a major profitability crisis for a manufacturing company. By building an end-to-end analytics platform, I identified ways to save $44,000 annually and fix critical inventory shortages


The ProblemThe organization was struggling with:Late Deliveries: Only 27% of orders arrived on time.High Costs: Expensive Air freight was being used for non-urgent shipments.Empty Shelves: 14 critical items were consistently out of stock.Broken Machines: Maintenance issues caused 23% of all production stops.


How I Built ItI followed an 8-phase analytics lifecycle to turn raw data into business strategy:Data Engineering: Built a central database in MySQL to house 7,000+ rows of supply chain records.SQL Analytics: Wrote advanced queries to rank the best and worst-performing suppliers.Data Science: Used Python to build Machine Learning models that predict if a shipment will be late (67% accuracy).Visual Dashboards: Created a 4-page Power BI dashboard to track costs and stock levels in real-time.


 Key Results
 $44,000  Potential Savings: Identified that switching 40% of shipments from Air to Sea would cut costs with no loss in speed.
 
 Risk Identification: Pinpointed 14 "High Risk" items that needed immediate reordering to prevent production stops.
 
 Supplier Accountability: Highlighted Supplier_10 as the primary bottleneck (85% late rate).
 Business Recommendations

Fix Logistics: Shift non-urgent cargo to Sea/Rail to save money.
Smarter Inventory: Increase "Safety Stock" for items with long lead times.

Better Contracts: Place low-performing suppliers on penalty-based contracts to improve reliability.
